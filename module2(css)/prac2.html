Garlic Soup

Garlic soup has been used for centuries as a home remedy, especially during cold and flu season. Its health benefits are largely due to allicin (a sulfur compound formed when garlic is crushed or chopped) and other antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals. Here are some key benefits:
Boosts Immunity – Garlic has natural antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal properties. It can help stimulate white blood cells and fight infections.
Reduces Cold & Flu Symptoms – Can help ease congestion, reduce severity of cold symptoms, and promote faster recovery.

Ingradients :

  • 2 whole heads of garlic (about 20–25 cloves), peeled
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2–3 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter
  • 6 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme or rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1 potato (for creaminess), a dash of turmeric, or a squeeze of lemon before serving

Instructions :

  1. Prepare Garlic: Peel all garlic cloves. For milder flavor, you can blanch them in boiling water for 2 minutes.
  2. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil/butter over medium heat. Add chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté for 5–7 minutes until softened.
  3. Add Garlic & Herbs: Add peeled garlic cloves, thyme/rosemary, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Sauté for another 2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Simmer: Pour in broth, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20–25 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  5. Blend: Remove bay leaf, then use an immersion blender or regular blender to purée until smooth.
  6. Adjust Seasoning: Taste and adjust salt/pepper as needed. For extra richness, stir in a spoonful of cream or coconut milk (optional).
  7. Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley, a drizzle of olive oil, or grated Parmesan.
